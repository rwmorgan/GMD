Phase 1 — ESC + ICT (Weeks 1–22)

All tasks are individual. Each task addresses criteria from both ESC205114 and ICT205114 simultaneously.

Unit 1 · Weeks 1–4

Digital Foundations

Before you build a game, you need to know your tools. This unit covers hardware, software identification, file management, digital citizenship, and OHS for screen-based work.

ESC C1ESC C2ESC C3ESC C4 ICT C1ICT C2
🛠 Tools: Word, Excel, Adobe Express, browser
Unit 2 · Weeks 5–9

Game Design & Documentation

Research a game genre, pitch a concept, write a full Game Design Document, and plan your project with a Gantt chart. The professional approach to starting a game.

ESC C2ESC C3 ICT C3ICT C5
🛠 Tools: Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Adobe Express
Unit 3 · Weeks 10–14

Asset Creation

Design sprites, tilesets, UI elements, and audio for your game. Learn copyright and attribution rules that apply to every asset you use — created or sourced.

ESC C1ESC C2ESC C3ESC C4 ICT C2ICT C3ICT C4
🛠 Tools: Photoshop, Illustrator, Audition, Adobe Express
Unit 4 · Weeks 15–19

Game Prototype

Set up your engine, import assets, and implement the core mechanic from your GDD. Build a playable prototype, test it yourself, and iterate based on what you find.

ESC C2ESC C3 ICT C1ICT C4ICT C5
🛠 Tools: Unity / Unreal / GMS2
Unit 5 · Weeks 20–22

Showcase & Review

Compile a portfolio of your Phase 1 work and present your prototype to the class. Collect feedback and reflect on your progress against the TASC criteria.

ESC C3ESC C4 ICT C2ICT C3ICT C5
🛠 Tools: Word, OneNote, PowerPoint, Adobe Express

Phase 2 — Project Implementation (Weeks 23–34)

Individual or pairs. TASC work requirements (project brief, Gantt, check-ins, team agreement, journal, peer/self eval) must all be completed.

Unit 6 · Weeks 23–34

Full Game Project

Take a new (or significantly expanded) game from concept to launch over 12 weeks. Define, plan, build, playtest, present, and publish your complete game. All 5 PRJ criteria.

PRJ C1PRJ C2PRJ C3PRJ C4PRJ C5
Individual or Pairs 8 work requirements
