Research, Copyright & References

How to research well, understand copyright in games, cite sources correctly, and maintain academic integrity throughout the course.

Copyright & Creative Commons

What is Copyright?

Copyright protects original creative work automatically when it is created. You cannot use someone else's art, music, code, or writing in your game without their permission — even if it's on the internet for free.

In Australia, copyright lasts for the lifetime of the creator + 70 years. There is no "copyright-free" internet.

How to Write Attribution Text

For CC BY assets, your credits screen must include something like:

"Explosion Sound" by SoundAuthor (freesound.org/s/12345/) licensed under CC BY 4.0

Each source's licence page usually provides the exact attribution text you should use.

APA Citation Guide

Website

Author, A. (Year, Month Day). Title of page. Website Name. URL

Article (no author)

Title of article. (Year). Website Name. URL

YouTube Video

ChannelName. (Year, Month Day). Title of video [Video]. YouTube. URL

GDC Talk

Speaker, A. (Year). Title of talk [Conference presentation]. Game Developers Conference, Location. URL

Recommended Research Sources

  • Game Developer Magazine ↗ — Professional articles, post-mortems
  • GDC Vault (free talks) ↗ — Design talks by professional developers
  • GamesIndustry.biz ↗ — Industry news and analysis
  • IGDB ↗ — Game database for release info
  • Wikipedia — OK as a starting point to find other sources. Do NOT cite Wikipedia in your bibliography.

Free Asset Sources with Good Licensing

  • OpenGameArt.org ↗ — Art and audio, most CC licensed
  • itch.io free assets ↗ — Filter by licence type
  • Kenney.nl ↗ — High-quality CC0 game assets (no attribution needed)
  • freesound.org ↗ — Community SFX. Filter by CC0 for no-attribution.
  • Google Fonts ↗ — Free fonts for UI. All OFL licensed (use freely).
