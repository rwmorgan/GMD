PRJ205118Phase 2 · Weeks 23–34 · Individual or Pairs

Project Implementation Level 2

5 criteria · 5 TCE credit points · Awards: SA / PA. All 6 TASC work requirements must be completed to be eligible for SA.

TASC Course Page ↗ Go to Unit 6 →

TASC Work Requirements Checklist

All 6 must be completed and submitted. Missing any of these results in a PA regardless of criterion ratings.

  • ☐ Project Brief — all sections completed, teacher sign-off (Task 6.1)
  • ☐ Time Schedule / Gantt Chart — Weeks 23–34, min 20 tasks (Task 6.2)
  • ☐ Team Work Agreement (pairs) or Working Agreement with Yourself (individual) (Task 6.1)
  • ☐ Documented Project Check-in Meetings × 3 — Weeks 27, 30, 33 (Task 6.3)
  • ☐ Journal — 500–800 words, individual, ongoing + summative reflection (Task 6.6)
  • ☐ Peer and Self Evaluation — individual (Task 6.7)
C1

Work productively to negotiate and complete tasks

▾
C — Satisfactory
  • Negotiates planning and completion of tasks with stakeholders to achieve project goals
  • Negotiates appropriate adaptations with stakeholders to meet specific needs/goals as required
  • Plans, prioritises and undertakes own work to achieve intended group outcomes
  • Follows established safety procedures for the use of equipment and facilities, as directed
  • Delivers upon agreed, achievable project goals within proposed timeframes
Assessed via
  • Project Brief (teacher sign-off = negotiation evidence)
  • Gantt chart updates at check-ins (planning and prioritisation)
  • Check-in documentation (adaptations made)
  • Final game build delivered by Week 34
Tasks: 6.1 6.2 6.3 6.4
C2

Communicate ideas and information

▾
C — Satisfactory
  • Clearly communicates — verbally, digitally and in writing — basic ideas and information related to the project
  • Develops, presents and explains ideas related to the project to different audiences
  • Uses digital media and technology to complete and review the project
  • Uses appropriate ICT tools to support reporting about the project
  • Incorporates and documents feedback and input into the project
Assessed via
  • Project Brief (written communication)
  • Check-in meeting reports (written + verbal)
  • Final presentation (verbal + digital)
  • itch.io game page (digital communication to public audience)
  • Feedback incorporation documented in check-ins
Tasks: 6.1 6.3 6.4 6.8
C3

Work collaboratively with others to implement a project

▾
C — Satisfactory
  • Correctly identifies own roles and responsibilities
  • Correctly identifies others' roles and responsibilities
  • Identifies and describes group goals
  • Documents consultation with relevant stakeholders
  • Identifies and utilises respectful communication strategies
Note for Individual pathway
  • Stakeholders include teacher and playtesters. Check-ins = documented consultation. Peer/Self Eval still required.
  • Respectful communication evidenced in check-in interactions and feedback response.
Tasks: 6.1 6.3 6.7
C4

Apply critical thinking and problem solving strategies

▾
C — Satisfactory
  • Sets goals which are generally measurable, achievable, specific, time-referenced and realistic
  • Identifies and utilises basic problem solving strategies
  • Identifies and poses questions that assist in producing outcomes of the project
  • Exercises flexibility and adaptability to meet changing conditions and achieve project goals
Assessed via
  • SMART goals in Project Brief
  • Bug fixes in playtesting (Task 6.5)
  • Gantt adaptations at check-ins
  • Risk register responses
Tasks: 6.1 6.2 6.4 6.5
C5

Reflect on thinking and processes

▾
C — Satisfactory
  • Explains how parts of the project interact with each other to produce project outcomes
  • Identifies and poses questions that clarify thinking that leads to better solutions
  • Assesses benefits of implementing a project by identifying risks, costs and effort involved
  • Identifies, utilises and documents problem solving techniques used to solve specific problems
  • Reflects on progress towards meeting goals and articulates ways goals can be met in the future
Assessed via
  • Journal entries (ongoing reflection)
  • Check-in retrospective sections
  • Peer/Self Evaluation + Project Value Assessment
  • Final presentation reflection section
  • Playtesting Quality Report
Tasks: 6.3 6.5 6.6 6.7 6.8
